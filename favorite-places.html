My Favorite Places

Below are some of my places in Lagos Nigeria

  • Landmark Beach

    Landmark Beach Lagos is a private, recreational beach resort in Victoria Island, Nigeria. It was founded by Paul Onwuanibe as a key part of the larger Landmark Village. The beach offers various leisure activities, from water sports to dining. Recently, a portion of the beach was demolished to make way for the Lagos-Calabar Coastal Highway.

    Landmark beach

  • Lekki Conservation Center

    Lekki Conservation Centre is a 78-hectare natural reserve that provides a peaceful escape from the busy city of Lagos. Known for having the longest canopy walkway in Africa, it offers visitors a chance to walk among the treetops and see wildlife like monkeys, crocodiles, and various bird species. It's a perfect place for nature lovers to enjoy a serene environment and learn about conservation.

    Lekki Conservation Center

  • Nike Art Gallery

    The Nike Art Gallery is one of the largest art galleries in West Africa and a true treasure trove of Nigerian art. Located in Lagos, the gallery is a five-story building filled with over 8,000 diverse artworks, including paintings, sculptures, and textiles. The gallery is a vibrant cultural hub that offers visitors an immersive experience in traditional and contemporary Nigerian art and provides a unique insight into the country's rich artistic traditions.

    Nike Art gallery
